An understanding of computer science is becoming increasingly essential in today’s world. Every job we're going to do in the future will rely on technology. Therefore, it is important that every student around the world be given the opportunity to learn computer science. By starting early, students will have an opportunity to create a foundation for success in any career path. Learning how to code programs stretches your mind, helps you think better, nurtures problem-solving skills, and will enhance your creativity.

According to Code.org, there were less than 50,000 Computer Science graduates in 2018. But there are over 500,000 open computing positions in the United States. If this rate continues, by 2025, the available seats for these positions will exceed qualified applicants by almost 2 million and the gap is continuing to rise. Based on the research, there are currently more than 900,000 computer related job openings in the US right now. Companies are finding it extremely challenging to find the right talent. To address this shortage many tech organizations are now turning to applicants with little to no experience and providing their own training programs for employees to fill these gaps

Comparing Computer Science Careers

Software Developers are the creative minds behind computer programs. Some develop applications that allow people to perform specific tasks on a computer or another device. Others develop the underlying systems that run the devices or control networks.

Computer hardware engineers are responsible for designing, developing, and testing computer components, such as circuit boards, routers, and memory devices. Computer hardware engineers need a combination of creativity and technical expertise. They must be avid learners who stay on top of emerging trends in the field to create hardware that can accommodate the latest programs and applications. Computer hardware engineers must have the perseverance to perform comprehensive tests of systems, again and again, to ensure the hardware is functioning properly.

Web developers build and shape visitors’ experiences at websites. They do this through the creation of page layouts (headings and paragraphs), website styling (colors and fonts), and page features (animations and pictures). Interactive features, such as submitting online payments securely, are a necessary feature of ecommerce sites.
